# Homepage Hero Academic-First Design

## Goal

Restructure the homepage hero so the first screen reads as a reliable academic homepage rather than a personal lifestyle landing page.

## Approved Direction

- Remove the casual portrait from the homepage hero entirely.
- Keep the hero as a two-column layout.
- Let the left column remain the single primary narrative area:
  - eyebrow
  - name
  - academic role
  - research summary
  - primary and secondary CTA buttons
- Move the dark academic spotlight card upward so it becomes the only right-column focal element.

## Content Hierarchy

### Left Column

The left column carries the homepage introduction and should remain the dominant reading path. It answers:

1. Who is this person?
2. What is their academic identity?
3. What research direction are they pursuing?
4. Where should the visitor go next?

### Right Column

The right column acts as a compact academic profile card rather than a second narrative block. It should contain:

- school or affiliation chip
- name
- one short academic summary
- advisor
- location
- three evidence metrics
- one short research note

The card should not repeat the full explanatory paragraph from the left column.

## Visual Principles

- One primary focal area per screen.
- No casual lifestyle image in the hero.
- The dark card should support academic credibility, not compete with the main copy.
- The hero height should shrink compared with the current stacked photo-plus-card layout.
- The first impression should feel formal, structured, and academically trustworthy.

## Out Of Scope

- No changes to the sections below the hero.
- No placement for the casual photo in this task.
- No change to the formal CV portrait layout.

## Acceptance Criteria

- The homepage hero no longer references `selfie.JPG`.
- The homepage hero no longer renders a separate portrait card.
- The academic spotlight card becomes the only right-column hero element.
- The site still builds successfully.
